Here is the Robbins-based and orthopaedically accurate evaluation of the question on fracture healing:

 

Question: Fracture healing — tick the correct answer(s):

Option

Evaluation

Explanation

A. Tibial plateau fractures are easily seen on plain x-rays

❌ Incorrect

Tibial plateau fractures can be difficult to visualize on plain radiographs, especially if non-displaced; CT scans are often needed for better assessment.

B. Tibial spine fractures are most commonly seen in 20–40 year old amateur athletes

❌ Incorrect

Tibial spine (intercondylar eminence) fractures are more commonly seen in children and adolescents, not in 20–40 year olds.

❌ Incorrect

❌ Incorrect

Robbins: Woven bone is laid down first in the callus and is later replaced by lamellar bone during the remodeling phase.

E. Callus contains osteoblasts and osteoclasts

✅ Correct

Robbins: “The callus consists of proliferating osteoblasts, and later, osteoclasts remodel the new bone.” This is a key part of fracture healing.

F. None of the above

❌ Incorrect

Because E is correct, this option is not valid.

 

✅ Correct Answer: E. Callus contains osteoblasts and osteoclasts

This is consistent with Robbins and standard bone healing physiology. The callus is an active site of bone formation and remodeling.
